Equilibrium Balance Mod Changelog

Welcome to the changelog for the Equilibrium balance mod. The purpose of this mod is to fix the existing issues with the game, and make improvements to gameplay by increasing its strategic diversity, while keeping the overall feel of the game the same and familiar, so you don't need to relearn the game to play this mod.

You don't need to read the whole changelog to be able to play, you can do exactly what you always do and it will work. Perhaps some of the things are not so strong as before but you can still pick all the usual strategies and be successful with them.

I put a lot of time and effort to bring you this mod, I hope you enjoy it as much as everyone else who has played it so far.

If you have any feedback, suggestions or complaints I will be happy to listen to you. However please back up your opinions of balance with replay evidence first.

-- Ithilis

General

Quick Summary

There are many changes in this mod, but don't worry, you need to remember only a few of them! The rest are designed to bring improvements while not compromising your gameplay.
  • Air factories cost more energy, but air is cheaper. Build 1-2 extra pgens before building an air factory!
  • Mercies deal damage over time! Moving targets receive only a fraction of the damage.
  • Mass storages are cheaper, provide less mass, and explode on death! Consider safety and use adjacency.
  • Energy storages, provide much more adjacency bonus, use them for fix your energy problem.
  • RAS energy income has been nerfed, don't reclaim your pgens afterwards!
  • T4 units take much longer to build. Get t2/3 engineers, they are more efficient in this mod.
  • Veterancy doesn't instant-heal units anymore, dont rely on that when attacking with experimentals or your ACU.

Veterancy

This is the first major change of the mod, which fixes many issues with veterancy and also allows it to become a larger part of the game rather than being mostly irrelevant for everything except T4 units and ACUs.

Here are the rules the new veterancy system as follows:

  • Units receive veterancy points that are equal to the destroyed unit's mass cost.
  • Units are promoted to next veterancy level, when they have 100% of their own mass cost in veterancy points
  • Destroying a unit with veterancy gives 20% more veterancy for each level - killing a tank with 1 star: 120% of its mass cost, killing a tank with 5 stars: 200% of its mass cost is awarded.
  • If multiple units kill the same target, the veterancy is shared out between them according to the fraction of the damage they dealt.

  • After promotion units receive new max health and gain higher regeneration, but don't heal for that value.
  • T1/T2/sACU units gain 20% more max hp.
  • T3/ACU units gain 15% more max hp.
  • Experimentals gain 10% more max HP.

  • All units gain increased regeneration depending on which category they are in:
  • Main Combat Units → The regen they get increases with each level
  • Raider/Support Units → The rise in regen is linear.

Regeneration values for Main Combat Units:

  • T1: +1+2+3+4+5 => 1/3/6/10/15
  • T2: +2+4+6+8+10 => 2/6/12/20/30

ACUs/Experimentals only need to destroy 50% of their own cost in mass worth of enemy units.

ACUs mass cost 2000m => 1000m required for vet = ~20T1 units to get veterancy but for destroying an ACU units gain 2000xp

Purpose

  • To avoid situations when destroying a T3 scout (195m) is worth the same as destroying a T3 battleship (9000m)
  • To add a system which is fair and predictable for each unit (units need to destroy 100% of their own mass to vet up)
  • You should no longer be required to self destruct lower tech units to prevent the enemy from gaining ridiculous amounts of experience
  • To fill new max hp faster and make units with veterancy more important overall. T3/4 units have less HP gains to promote using lower tech units against higher tech ones. Experimentals vet up faster but don't gain as much from doing so.
  • Splitting veterancy rewards ensures that units gain vet much more evenly rather than having one 5 vet loyalist after an exp battle.
  • The regeneration helps different units gain the most from veterancy values - units which won't live long get regen faster than units which do, helping to highlight their roles and making them more varied.
  • Naval units and submarines are far more expensive than land units and have more HP so they also gain more regeneration.

Reclaim

1. The time needed to reclaim something is 2x longer.
  • A unit with a build rate of 1 reclaims at: 10m/s → 5m/s

2. The sACU wreck is reclaimable 2x slower than other units.

3. Megalith’s egg reclaim abuse 1800m/s → 200m/s

4. Air experimental units wrecks gain 81% mass → 40.5% mass

Purpose

  1. To make it harder to secure a reclaim field after a fight, meaning that the map becomes more dynamic as there are reclaim fields to fight over for longer.
  2. Taking more time to be reclaimed, sACU spam will no longer instantly be converted into mass as soon as one of them dies.

  4. Air experimentals leave half of their mass in wrecks now - this is because they are likely to die quickly and this decreases the amount of mass delivery. Its similar reason as on ASF - be more free to use them on enemy territory.

Land

In this mod land units are adjusted to keep the same purposes but to make gameplay more consistent (less random BS) and make tech level progression not so steep - so you can use lower tech units more effectively later on, rather than having to crtl-k them for mass.
  • T1 units have more hp, and less dps, in total with the same efficiency, but give more time while they are being killed = can be used as a meat shield for higher tech armies that have a larger range
  • T2 units have more concentrated dps in one unit, and have units with special roles
  • T3 units have even more concentrated dps and hp in one unit than T2, and have additional units for special roles.
  • T4 experimentals have a new build-time, which is 33% of their mass spent on units built from a T3 factory, before was experimental build-time was equal to ~12%.
